Outliers

"For unto everyone that hath shall be given, and he shall have abundance. But from him that hath not shall be taken away even that which he hath"

Key-points

The Secret of High Achievers: “Ericsson and his colleagues then compared amateur pianists with professional painters. The same pattern emerged. The amateurs never practiced more than about three hours a week throughout their childhood, and by the age of twenty, they had totaled two thousand hours of practice. On the other hand, the professionals steadily increased their practice time every year until, like the violinists, they had reached ten thousand hours by the age of twenty. The striking thing about Ericsson’s study is that he and his colleagues couldn’t find any “naturals,” musicians who floated effortlessly to the top while practicing a fraction of the time their peers did. Nor could they find any “grinds,” people who worked harder than everyone else yet just didn’t have what it takes to break the top ranks. Their research suggested that once a musician has enough ability to get into a top music school, distinguishing one performer from another is how hard they work. That’s it. And what’s more, the people at the very top don’t work just harder or even much harder than everyone else. They work much, much harder. The idea that excellence at performing a complex task requires a critical minimum level of practice surfaces again and again in studies of experts. Researchers have settled on what they believe is the magic number for true expertise: 10000Hours. The emerging picture from such studies is that ten thousand hours of practice is required to achieve the level of mastery associated with being a world-class expert. It seems that it takes the brain this long to assimilate all it needs to know to attain a true form of mastery.”

Mathew Principle: It is those who are successful, in other words, are most likely to be given the kinds of exceptional opportunities that lead to further success.

Practice: Practice isn't the thing you do once you are good. It's the only thing you do that makes you good. You become good at something when you do it consistently. If you put in the time and the work daily on something, it becomes a habit. Imagine if you counted the hours spent working on the things you do daily. The secret of the future is hidden in our daily routine.
